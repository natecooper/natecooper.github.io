Turtles are reptiles known for their distinctive shells, which provide protection from predators. They belong to the order Testudines and have existed for millions of years, making them some of the oldest reptiles on Earth.

There are both freshwater and marine turtles. Marine turtles, such as the green sea turtle and the leatherback, migrate long distances across oceans. Freshwater turtles are more commonly found in lakes, rivers, and swamps.

Many turtle species are endangered due to habitat loss, pollution, and illegal hunting. Conservation efforts worldwide aim to protect their natural habitats and ensure their survival for future generations.
